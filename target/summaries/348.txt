Njau v Republic

Division: High Court of Kenya at Nairobi

Date of judgment: 25 March 1974

Case Number: 162/1973 (66/74)

Before: Trevelyan and Muli JJ

Sourced by: LawAfrica

[1] Criminal Practice & Procedure  Sentence  Dangerous driving  Causing death  Custodial

sentence substituted for fine.

Editors Summary

The appellant was convicted of causing death by dangerous driving and was sentenced to pay a fine. The

facts upheld by the High Court showed deliberate risk-taking by the appellant while driving a lorry load

of boys.

On appeal.

Held  in such circumstances a custodial sentence must be imposed (Wanjema v. Republic (1) and Khalif

v. Republic (2) considered).

Sentence of 3 years imprisonment substituted.

Cases referred to Judgment:

(1) Wanjema v. Republic, [1971] E.A. 493.
