BERONDA V UGANDA

Division: Court of Appeal at Kampala

Date of judgment: 31 January 1974

Case Number: 117/1973 (15/74)

Before: Sir William Duffus P, Spry V-P and Mustafa JA

Sourced by: LawAfrica

Appeal from: High Court of Uganda  Musoke, J

[1] Evidence  Confession  Recorded by magistrate alone  Proceeding proper  Police officers

presence undesirable  Evidence Act (Cap. 43), s. 24 (U.).

Editors Summary

A confession had been made to a magistrate who was alone with the accused apart from the interpreter.

The trial judge stated that a police officer should have been present.

Held 

(i) The procedure adopted by the magistrate was wholly correct;

( ii) it is undesirable that a police officer should be present while a confession is recorded.

Order accordingly.

No cases referred to in judgment
