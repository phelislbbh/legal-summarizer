Nganga v Republic

Division: High Court of Kenya at Nairobi

Date of judgment: 31 October 1974

Case Number: 412/1974 (9/75)

Before: Sir James Wicks CJ and Trevelyan J

Sourced by: LawAfrica

[1] Criminal Practice and Procedure  Sentence  Rogue and vagabond  Increased sentence only allowed when previous offence under the section  Penal Code, s. 183 (K.).

Editors Summary

The appellant was sentenced to 12 months imprisonment for being a rogue and vagabond. None of his previous convictions were for this offence.

Held  an increased sentence can only be awarded when there is a previous conviction of being a rogue and vagabond.

Sentence reduced.
