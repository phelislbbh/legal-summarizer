Uganda v Karasa

Division: High Court of Uganda at Kampala

Date of judgment: 5 April 1974

Case Number: 84/1974 (125/74)

Before: Wambuzi CJ

Sourced by: LawAfrica

[1] Criminal Practice and Procedure  Compensation  Substantial compensation  What sum is

substantial  Magistrates Court Act 1970, s. 209 (U.)

Editors Summary

The accused was ordered to pay Shs. 42/- compensation for trees he was convicted of stealing.

Held  substantial compensation means a relatively large sum of money and Shs. 42/- cannot be such a

sum.

Order set aside.
