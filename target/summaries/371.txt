Ombanya v Gailey & Roberts Ltd  Division: High Court of Kenya at Nairobi  Date of judgment: 2 December 1971  Case Number: 358/1971 (14/75)  Before: Muli J  Sourced by: LawAfrica  [1] Damages  Master and servant  Wrongful dismissal  Wages for period during which notice would  run only recoverable  General damages not awarded.  Editors Summary  The plaintiff was held to have been wrongfully dismissed by the defendant. He claimed a months salary  in lieu of notice, severance pay, leave pay and general damages for loss of employment. The claim for  general damages was disputed.  Held  where there is a period for termination of a contract the damages suffered are the wages for the  period during which normal notice would have been current.  Claim for general damages disallowed.  Cases referred to Judgment:  (1) Kyobe v. East African Airways, H.C.C.C. 268 of 1970 (unreported).  (2) Lukenya Ranching v. Kavoloto, [1970] E.A. 414.
