Uganda v Omong  Division: High Court of Uganda at Kampala  Date of judgment: 4 April 1974  Case Number: 8/1974 (128/74)  Before: Manyindo J  Sourced by: LawAfrica  [1] Criminal Law  Failure to prevent spread of fire  Prevention must have been possible  Penal  Code, s. 311 (U.).  Editors Summary  The accused was convicted of having failed to prevent the extension to other land of a fire lit by him. The statement of facts showed that the fire had been carried by the wind.  Held  the spread of the fire must have been preventable for an offence to be committed.  Order accordingly.  No cases referred to in judgment
